# Universal Proxy

A **universal proxy card** lists **all director nominees from all sides** in a contested election, so a shareholder voting by proxy can mix-and-match candidates exactly as they could in person. Required by **Rule 14a-19**, adopted **November 17, 2021**. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Universal Proxy]])

---

## Scope and dates

- Mandatory in **all non-exempt director election contests** for operating companies (registered investment companies and BDCs are excluded). (Source: [[SEC.gov - Universal Proxy]])
- **Effective January 31, 2022**; applies to any shareholder meeting with a director election **after August 31, 2022**.
- Adopting release: **34-93596**.

## Key rule mechanics (verified from the SEC guide)

- **Rule 14a-19(e):** the card must name **all nominees of each side** (including proxy-access nominees), grouped by nominating party, alphabetical by last name within each group, in a uniform font/style, and must state the maximum number that may be voted for.
- **Bona fide nominee redefined (Rule 14a-4(c)(5)):** now includes any party's nominee, which is what lets each side print the other side's names on its card.
- **Eliminated the "short slate rule"** for operating companies (it remains for funds/BDCs).
- **Dissident deadlines (Rule 14a-19(a)-(d)):** notify the registrant of nominees **60 days** before the prior year's meeting anniversary; the registrant notifies the dissident of its nominees **50 days** before; the dissident files a definitive proxy statement by the later of **25 days** before the meeting or 5 days after the registrant's; and must **solicit holders of at least 67%** of the voting power and say so.

## Why it matters

It lowers the barrier for activist investors: a dissident no longer needs to run a full competing card, since shareholders can pick individual nominees across both slates. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Universal Proxy]])
