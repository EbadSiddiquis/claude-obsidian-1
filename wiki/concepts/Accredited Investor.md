# Accredited Investor

An **accredited investor** is a person or entity the SEC deems able to bear the risk of unregistered private offerings (e.g., [[Regulation D]] Rule 506). Accreditation is the gatekeeper for most private capital markets. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Accredited Investor Definition Amendments]])

---

## Individual thresholds

- **Income:** over **$200,000** individually (or **$300,000** with spouse/partner) in each of the prior two years, with expectation of the same. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Accredited Investor Definition Amendments]])
- **Net worth:** over **$1 million**, excluding primary residence (individually or jointly).

## 2020 modernization

Effective **December 8, 2020**, the SEC added a **knowledge-based** path: individuals holding certain professional credentials in good standing qualify regardless of wealth. The SEC designated the **Series 7, Series 65, and Series 82** licenses as qualifying. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Accredited Investor Definition Amendments]])

> [!note] Legislation to further expand accredited-investor eligibility (e.g., a knowledge/exam-based test) has advanced in Congress; status as of mid-2026 is a moving target.

## Why it matters

The definition sets who can invest in Rule 506(c) (accredited-only) deals and shapes the verification burden under [[Regulation D]]. Broadening it expands the private-capital investor pool.

## Confidence

**high** on the income/net-worth thresholds and the 2020 credential additions; **medium** on pending congressional expansion (in flux).
