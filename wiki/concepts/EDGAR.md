# EDGAR

**EDGAR** (Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval) is the [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|SEC]]'s electronic system for the collection, validation, indexing, acceptance, and public dissemination of mandatory corporate filings. (Source: [[Wikipedia - EDGAR]])

---

## What it does

EDGAR automates the receipt, processing, and distribution of registration statements, periodic reports, ownership filings, and other documents that companies are required by law to file. It exists to increase the efficiency and fairness of securities markets by speeding the public's access to time-sensitive corporate information. (Source: [[SEC.gov - About EDGAR]])

## Scale

Verified directly from sec.gov (2026-06-29, compliant User-Agent request):

- Processes about **4,700 filings per day** (Source: [[SEC.gov - About EDGAR]])
- Serves up **3,000 terabytes** of data to the public annually
- Accommodates **40,000 new filers per year** on average
- Holds **17+ million filings** as of May 2025 (Source: [[Wikipedia - EDGAR]])

> [!note] Different sources cite different headline counts (e.g., "36 million documents," "17 million filings"). The discrepancy is documents vs. filing submissions - a single filing contains multiple documents.

## History

- Development began **1993** (Source: [[Wikipedia - EDGAR]])
- Mandatory for domestic public companies on **May 6, 1996**, after a three-year phase-in
- Foreign companies and governments required to file via EDGAR on **November 4, 2002**
- **2017:** SEC disclosed the database had been breached, with stolen data potentially used for insider trading
- **2024–2025:** [[EDGAR Next]] modernization - Login.gov authentication and role-based access

## Access & formats

- Public access is free at `edgar.sec.gov` - no subscription required (Source: [[SEC.gov - About EDGAR]])
- Accepted formats: HTML, ASCII (.txt), XML, and XBRL
- Available for filing 6 a.m.–10 p.m. ET on weekdays (excluding federal holidays)
