# SEC Enforcement Process

The SEC's **Division of Enforcement** investigates potential securities-law violations and decides whether to recommend charges. The hallmark procedural step is the **Wells process**. (Source: [[Winston & Strawn - SEC Investigations Primer]])

---

## The flow

1. **Investigation** (often informal -> formal order), with document subpoenas and testimony.
2. **Wells notice** - staff formally notify a subject that they intend to recommend an enforcement action, identifying the suspected violations.
3. **Wells submission** - the subject may respond in writing arguing why charges should not be brought. (Recent reforms give **four weeks** instead of two, and require an Associate Director or above at post-Wells meetings.) (Source: [[Winston & Strawn - SEC Investigations Primer]])
4. **Decision** - staff recommend charges or close the investigation.

## Two forums

An enforcement action proceeds either as:
- an **administrative proceeding** before the SEC (in-house), or
- a **civil action in federal court**.

## Settlement

Most matters settle. Recent reforms **separate settlement discussions from Wells submissions** - staff reject Wells submissions that discuss settlement terms or invoke FRE Rule 408; settlement offers must be made separately. (Source: [[Winston & Strawn - SEC Investigations Primer]])

## Remedies

Disgorgement, civil penalties, injunctions, officer-and-director bars, and (for regulated entities) suspensions or revocations.

## Confidence

**high** on the Wells-process structure and the two forums; **medium** on the precise 2025-2026 procedural reforms (recent, evolving).
