# SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure Rules

Adopted **July 26, 2023**, these rules require public companies to disclose material cybersecurity incidents promptly and to describe their cyber risk-management and governance annually. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Cybersecurity Disclosure Rules Press Release]])

---

## Two pieces

**1. Form 8-K Item 1.05 - incident disclosure.**
- File within **4 business days** after determining a cybersecurity incident is **material**. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Cybersecurity Disclosure Rules Press Release]])
- Describe the material aspects of the incident's nature, scope, and timing, and its material (or reasonably likely material) impact.
- Disclosure may be **delayed** if the U.S. Attorney General determines immediate disclosure poses a substantial risk to national security or public safety.
- If information is unavailable at filing, say so and file an amended 8-K within 4 business days of learning it.
- A "cybersecurity incident" includes **a series of related unauthorized occurrences**, so aggregated related intrusions can trigger Item 1.05 even if each alone seems immaterial.

**2. Regulation S-K Item 106 - annual disclosure.**
- Annual (10-K) reporting on cybersecurity **risk management, strategy, governance, and board oversight**. Does not mandate disclosing board members' specific cyber expertise.

## Significance

This is one of the few major 2023-2026 SEC disclosure rules that took effect and **stuck** (unlike the [[Recent SEC Rulemaking and Litigation 2023-2026|vacated buyback rule and stayed climate rule]]). It made "materiality determination" timing a live compliance question for issuers.
