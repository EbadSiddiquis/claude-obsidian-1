# Regulation S-X

**Regulation S-X** (17 CFR Part 210) prescribes the **form and content of the financial statements** filed with the SEC. Where [[Regulation S-K]] is narrative judgment, S-X is a **technical rule** that works alongside US GAAP. (Source: [[CFO Bridge - Regulation S-X vs S-K]])

---

## What it governs

- The **form, content, footnotes, and schedules** of financial statements, ensuring consistency and comparability. (Source: [[Cornell LII - Regulation S-X]])
- Applies across the Securities Act, Exchange Act, Investment Company Act, and Investment Advisers Act.
- **Auditor independence** standards.
- **Acquired-business financials** (Rule 3-05) and **pro forma** information (Article 11).

## Audited periods & significance tests

- The number of audited periods required (e.g., one vs two years) is driven by **significance tests** under **Rule 1-02(w)** (asset, income, or investment tests). (Source: [[Cornell LII - Regulation S-X]])
- **Scaled relief** exists for Smaller Reporting Companies (SRCs) and Emerging Growth Companies (EGCs).

## Relationship to S-K and XBRL

S-X defines the statements; [[Regulation S-K]] wraps them in narrative; and those statements are tagged in [[XBRL]] for EDGAR.

## Confidence

**high** - S-X's role, the 3-05 acquired-business rule, and the 1-02(w) significance tests are codified.
