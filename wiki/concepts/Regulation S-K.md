# Regulation S-K

**Regulation S-K** is the SEC's rulebook for **narrative, non-financial disclosure** - the "storytelling" half of a filing. It defines the content of the business description, risk factors, MD&A, and executive compensation. (Source: [[CFO Bridge - Regulation S-X vs S-K]])

---

## What it governs

- **Business** overview, **risk factors**, properties, legal proceedings.
- **MD&A (Item 303)** - Management's Discussion and Analysis: a narrative that lets investors "see the company through the eyes of management," covering known **trends, demands, commitments, events, and uncertainties**, plus liquidity, capital resources, and critical accounting estimates. (Source: [[Cornell LII - Regulation S-K]])
- **Executive compensation**, corporate governance, and related-party disclosures.

## MD&A modernization (2020)

In December 2020 the SEC amended Item 303 toward a more **principles-based** MD&A, reducing duplicative disclosure and codifying prior guidance. (Source: [[CFO Bridge - Regulation S-X vs S-K]])

## Relationship to S-X

Reg S-K (narrative) and [[Regulation S-X]] (financial statements) are the two halves of a complete 10-K or registration statement; they are filed through one coordinated process. (Source: [[CFO Bridge - Regulation S-X vs S-K]])
