# T+1 Settlement Cycle

A market-structure rule (amended **Rule 15c6-1**) that shortened the standard securities settlement cycle from two business days (**T+2**) to one (**T+1**). Adopted Feb 15, 2023; effective **May 28, 2024**. (Source: [[Davis Polk - SEC Adopts T+1 Settlement]])

---

- **What it covers:** most securities transactions - stocks, bonds, ETFs, certain mutual funds, and exchange-traded limited partnerships. (Source: [[Davis Polk - SEC Adopts T+1 Settlement]])
- **Exemptions:** certain government bonds, commercial paper, and some limited-partnership interests.
- **Why:** reduces credit, market, and liquidity risk from unsettled trades.
- **Compliance date nuance:** May 28, 2024 (the Tuesday after Memorial Day), a two-month extension from the proposal.

Unlike the contested disclosure rules of the same era, T+1 is a plumbing change that took effect smoothly and is now the operating standard.
