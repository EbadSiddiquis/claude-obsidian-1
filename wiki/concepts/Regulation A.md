# Regulation A

**Regulation A** (often "Reg A+" after the 2015 JOBS Act expansion) is an exemption that lets companies make a **public-facing offering to anyone** (including non-accredited investors) without a full registration - a "mini-IPO." (Source: [[SEC.gov - Regulation A]])

---

## Two tiers

| | Tier 1 | Tier 2 |
|---|---|---|
| 12-month cap | up to **$20M** | up to **$75M** |
| SEC qualification (Form 1-A) | Yes | Yes |
| State (blue-sky) review | Yes | **Preempted** (no state qualification) |
| Ongoing reporting | Limited | Annual/semiannual + current event reports |
| Affiliate-sale cap | $6M | $22.5M |

(Source: [[SEC.gov - Regulation A]])

- The offering statement is filed on **Form 1-A** and must be **qualified** by SEC staff before sales.
- Tier 2's state preemption is its key advantage; the trade-off is ongoing reporting.

## History

Adopted **March 25, 2015** (effective June 19, 2015) under the JOBS Act. In **March 2021** the Tier 2 cap rose from $50M to **$75M**. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Regulation A]])

## Confidence

**high** - tier caps, Form 1-A qualification, and state preemption are codified and corroborated.
