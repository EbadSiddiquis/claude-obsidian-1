# Proxy Statement

A **proxy statement** is the disclosure document a company must give shareholders when soliciting their votes, under **Section 14(a)** of the [[Federal Securities Laws|Securities Exchange Act of 1934]] and **Regulation 14A**. The definitive version is filed on EDGAR as **DEF 14A**. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Proxy Rules and Schedules 14A 14C]])

---

## What counts as a "solicitation"

Per SEC staff interpretation, a solicitation is **"any communication to security holders under circumstances reasonably calculated to result in the procurement, withholding or revocation of a proxy."** That broad definition means even informal communications can trigger the proxy rules. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Proxy Rules and Schedules 14A 14C]])

## What's in it (Schedule 14A)

Typical annual-meeting agenda items disclosed:
- **Election of directors**
- **Ratification of the auditor**
- **Say-on-pay** advisory vote (see below)
- Executive compensation detail (driven by [[Regulation S-K]])
- Other matters (plan approvals, charter amendments)

## Say-on-pay

A shareholder **advisory** vote on executive compensation, required by the **Dodd-Frank Act**. It is **non-binding** - a company is not legally required to change pay even if shareholders vote against it. The advisory materials are themselves a solicitation subject to Section 14(a) and Regulation 14A. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Proxy Rules and Schedules 14A 14C]])

## Related mechanics

- **[[Universal Proxy]]** (Rule 14a-19): contested director elections must use one card listing all sides' nominees.
- **[[Rule 14a-8 Shareholder Proposals]]**: how a shareholder gets a proposal into the company's own proxy.
