# Tender Offer

A **tender offer** is a public, broad offer to buy shareholders' securities, usually at a premium, to acquire control. It is governed by the [[Williams Act]] (Sections 14(d)/14(e)) and the SEC's Regulations 14D and 14E. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Tender Offer Rules and Schedules]])

---

## Key filings

- **Schedule TO** - the bidder's filing. EDGAR tags distinguish types: **SC TO-T** (third-party offer), **SC TO-I** (issuer self-tender), **SC TO-C** (pre-commencement communications). (Source: [[SEC.gov - Tender Offer Rules and Schedules]])
- **Schedule 14D-9** - the **target board's** required response, filed within **10 business days** of the offer.

## Core procedural rules

- **Minimum offering period:** **20 business days** open (Rule 14e-1), historically, so holders have time to decide.
- **Withdrawal rights:** holders may withdraw tendered shares while the offer is open (Rule 14d-7).
- **All-holders rule:** the offer must be open to **every** holder of the class on identical terms.
- **Best-price rule:** every tendering holder must receive the **highest** price paid to any holder.
- **Rule 14e-8:** a bidder must have a **bona fide intent** to commence once a Schedule TO is filed.

## Related regimes

- **Rule 13e-4** - **issuer** tender offers (a company buying back its own shares).
- **Rule 13e-3** - **going-private** transactions; triggers heightened disclosure.

## 2026 change (notable)

On **April 16, 2026**, the SEC's Division of Corporation Finance issued an **exemptive order** halving the minimum period to **10 business days** for **negotiated, all-cash** equity tender offers under certain conditions - a meaningful M&A-speed change from the long-standing 20-day floor. (Source: [[Ropes & Gray - SEC Cuts Minimum Tender Offer Period]])

## Confidence

**high** on the Schedule TO/14D-9 mechanics, the 20-day floor, and all-holders/best-price (SEC primary + multiple law firms); **high** on the April 2026 10-day order (corroborated by Ropes & Gray, Freshfields, Dechert).
