# Regulation D

**Regulation D** is the most-used set of exemptions from Securities Act registration for **private placements**. Its dominant safe harbor is **Rule 506**, in two flavors. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Private Placements Rule 506]])

---

## Rule 506(b) vs Rule 506(c)

| | Rule 506(b) | Rule 506(c) |
|---|---|---|
| General solicitation/advertising | **Not allowed** | **Allowed** |
| Who can buy | Unlimited accredited + up to **35** non-accredited (sophisticated) | **Accredited only** |
| Verification of accreditation | "Reasonable belief" (self-certification OK) | "Reasonable steps to verify" (review docs / third-party confirmation) |
| Amount raised | Unlimited | Unlimited |

(Source: [[Carta - 506(b) vs 506(c) Guide]])

- Non-accredited investors in a 506(b) deal must be **sophisticated** and receive disclosure documents similar to a Reg A offering.
- 506(c)'s general-solicitation freedom is the trade-off for the stricter verification duty.

## Form D

Issuers using Rule 506 do **not** register the offering, but must file a **Form D** electronically with the SEC within **15 days** of the first sale. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Private Placements Rule 506]])

## Place in the landscape

Reg D is the workhorse of private capital. See [[Exempt Offerings]] for how it compares to [[Regulation A]] (public-facing "mini-IPO") and [[Regulation Crowdfunding]] (retail crowdfunding).

## Confidence

**high** - the 506(b)/506(c) distinctions and Form D timing are codified and corroborated across SEC and law sources.
