# Funding Portal

A **funding portal** is an SEC-registered, FINRA-member intermediary that hosts [[Regulation Crowdfunding]] offerings online. It is a lighter-weight alternative to a registered broker-dealer, created specifically for Reg CF. (Source: [[FINRA - Funding Portals]])

---

## Registration path

- Register with the SEC by filing **Form Funding Portal** through [[EDGAR]], and become a **member of [[Financial Industry Regulatory Authority|FINRA]]**. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Registration of Funding Portals]])
- FINRA tracks portals in the **Funding Portal Registration Depository (FPRD)** and publishes a list of regulated portals.
- Governed by FINRA's **Funding Portal Rules** (Rule 100 general standards, 110 applications, 200 conduct, 300 compliance, 800 investigations, 900 procedure, 1200 arbitration). (Source: [[FINRA - Funding Portals]])
- Must report gross revenue annually on **Form FP-Statement of Revenue** within 60 days of year-end.

## What a funding portal may NOT do

Unlike a broker-dealer, a funding portal **cannot**: (Source: [[MicroVentures - Funding Portals vs Broker-Dealers]])

- Offer investment advice or recommendations
- Solicit purchases or actively promote specific offerings
- Hold, manage, or possess investor funds or securities
- Pay employees/agents transaction-based compensation tied to sales

## Portal vs broker-dealer

| | Funding portal | Broker-dealer |
|---|---|---|
| Advice/recommendations | No | Yes |
| Solicitation | No | Yes |
| Hold investor funds | No | Yes |
| Transaction-based comp | No | Yes |
| Regulatory burden | Lighter | Heavier (KYC/AML, full FINRA rules) |

A broker-dealer can do more (active promotion, guidance) but carries heavier compliance; a funding portal is a passive marketplace with a lighter footprint. (Source: [[MicroVentures - Funding Portals vs Broker-Dealers]])

## Confidence

**high** — funding-portal scope, prohibitions, and registration path are codified in SEC/FINRA rules and corroborated across sources.
