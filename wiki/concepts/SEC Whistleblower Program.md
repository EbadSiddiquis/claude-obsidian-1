# SEC Whistleblower Program

Established by the **Dodd-Frank Act (2010)**, the program pays monetary awards to people who voluntarily provide original information leading to a successful SEC enforcement action. (Source: [[Wikipedia - SEC Office of the Whistleblower]])

---

## How it works

- **Award range: 10% to 30%** of monetary sanctions collected, but only when sanctions **exceed $1 million**. (Source: [[Wikipedia - SEC Office of the Whistleblower]])
- Must be **voluntary, original information** that **leads to** a successful action.
- Awards are paid from the **Investor Protection Fund** (funded by collected sanctions) - **not** from money owed to harmed investors.

## Award factors

- **Increase:** significance of the information, assistance provided, law-enforcement interest, use of internal compliance.
- **Decrease:** the whistleblower's own culpability, unreasonable reporting delay, interference with internal compliance.

## Anti-retaliation

Dodd-Frank prohibits employers from firing, demoting, suspending, threatening, harassing, or discriminating against someone who reports to the SEC.

## Confidence

**high** - the 10-30% range, $1M threshold, and Investor Protection Fund are codified in Dodd-Frank and SEC rules.
