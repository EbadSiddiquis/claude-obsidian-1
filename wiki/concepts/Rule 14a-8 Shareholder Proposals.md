# Rule 14a-8 Shareholder Proposals

**Rule 14a-8** is the process by which an eligible shareholder can require a company to include the shareholder's **own proposal** in the company's [[Proxy Statement]], to be voted on at the annual meeting. (Source: [[Debevoise - Shareholder Proposals under Rule 14a-8]])

---

## How it works

- An eligible holder submits a proposal; the company must include it **unless** it qualifies for one of the rule's **exclusion grounds** (e.g., improper under state law, relates to ordinary business operations, personal grievance, or duplicates another proposal).
- To exclude a proposal, the company typically seeks a **no-action letter** from the SEC's Division of Corporation Finance.
- Eligibility and resubmission thresholds (ownership amount/duration, prior-vote support) gate who can submit and re-submit.

## Distinct from universal proxy

Rule 14a-8 (getting a *proposal* onto the company's proxy) is separate from **[[Universal Proxy|Rule 14a-19]]** (getting *director nominees* onto a single card in a contest). Activists gained easier board access via universal proxy **without** the ownership minimums and subject-matter guardrails of the 14a-8 system. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Proxy Rules and Schedules 14A 14C]])
