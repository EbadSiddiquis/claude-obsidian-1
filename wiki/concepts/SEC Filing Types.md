# SEC Filing Types

Public companies file standardized forms with the [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|SEC]] through [[EDGAR]]. Each form serves a distinct disclosure purpose under the [[Federal Securities Laws]]. (Source: [[edgar.tools - SEC Filing Types Explained]])

---

## Periodic reports (Exchange Act of 1934)

- **10-K - Annual report.** Comprehensive overview of business and financial condition, with **audited** financial statements. Large accelerated filers: within 60 days of fiscal year-end; non-accelerated filers: 90 days. (Source: [[edgar.tools - SEC Filing Types Explained]])
- **10-Q - Quarterly report.** Filed for each of the first three fiscal quarters within ~40–45 days of quarter-end; contains **unaudited** financials.
- **8-K - Current report.** Discloses 30+ types of material events (acquisitions, executive changes, financial results, delistings) within **4 business days** of the triggering event.

## Registration statements (Securities Act of 1933)

- **S-1 - Registration statement.** Used to register securities for a public offering (e.g., an IPO). Goes through iterative SEC review: initial filing → SEC comments → amendments. (Source: [[edgar.tools - SEC Filing Types Explained]])
- **S-3** - Streamlined registration for established, seasoned issuers.

## Ownership & control

- **Schedule 13D / 13G** - Beneficial ownership disclosure required of anyone acquiring more than **5%** of a company's securities. (Source: [[investor.gov - Laws That Govern the Securities Industry]])
- **Forms 3, 4, 5** - Insider (officer/director/10% owner) ownership and transaction reports.

## Proxy & funds

- **DEF 14A** - Definitive proxy statement for shareholder meetings.
- **13F** - Quarterly holdings report for institutional investment managers.

## Confidence

**high** - form purposes and deadlines are corroborated by multiple sources and codified in SEC rules. Specific day-count deadlines can vary by filer category; verify against current SEC rules for edge cases.
