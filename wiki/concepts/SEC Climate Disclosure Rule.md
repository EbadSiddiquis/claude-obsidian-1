# SEC Climate Disclosure Rule

A March 2024 rule that would have required public companies to disclose material climate-related risks and certain greenhouse-gas emissions. It was **stayed, never enforced, and is now being rescinded** - a case study in post-2024 SEC rulemaking reversal. (Source: [[DLA Piper - SEC Climate Rule Litigation Update]])

---

## Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Mar 2024 | SEC adopts climate-related disclosure rules |
| Apr 4, 2024 | SEC **stays** the rules pending Eighth Circuit litigation (Iowa v. SEC) |
| Mar 27, 2025 | SEC votes to **end its defense** of the rules |
| Apr 2025 | Litigation held in abeyance |
| Sep 12, 2025 | Eighth Circuit holds petitions in abeyance until SEC reconsiders or renews defense |
| 2026 | SEC **proposes rescission** of the rules in their entirety (asserting they exceed statutory authority) |

(Source: [[DLA Piper - SEC Climate Rule Litigation Update]], [[SEC.gov - Proposes Rescission of Climate Rules]])

## Net effect

The rule never took effect; companies were never required to comply. The proposed rescission is expected to effectively end the litigation. The episode reflects a broader pullback from contested disclosure mandates under a changed Commission.

## Confidence

**high** on the litigation/abeyance/rescission sequence (SEC statements + multiple law-firm trackers agree); **medium** on the precise wording of the 2026 rescission proposal pending the final Federal Register text.
