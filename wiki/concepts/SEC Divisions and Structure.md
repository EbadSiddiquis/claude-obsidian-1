# SEC Divisions and Structure

The [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|SEC]] is led by a five-member Commission and organized into **five operating divisions** plus supporting offices. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Organizational Chart]])

---

## The five divisions

1. **Corporation Finance** - oversees public-company disclosure (reviews [[SEC Filing Types|registration statements and periodic reports]]; issues comment letters). (Source: [[SEC.gov - Organizational Chart]])
2. **Enforcement** - investigates securities-law violations and brings actions (see [[SEC Enforcement Process]]).
3. **Trading and Markets** - regulates exchanges, brokers, dealers, clearing agencies, and market structure.
4. **Investment Management** - oversees mutual funds, investment companies, and investment advisers.
5. **Economic and Risk Analysis (DERA)** - the SEC's "think tank" (created 2009); provides economic, statistical, and risk-modeling analysis across rulemaking, examination, and enforcement.

## Note

FINRA ([[Financial Industry Regulatory Authority]]) is a **self-regulatory organization** overseen by the SEC, not an SEC division. The SEC supervises FINRA; FINRA front-line-regulates broker-dealers and [[Funding Portal|funding portals]].
