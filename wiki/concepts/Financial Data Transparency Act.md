# Financial Data Transparency Act

The **FDTA** (enacted December 2022 as part of P.L. 117-263) directs nine federal financial regulators to jointly adopt **uniform, machine-readable data standards** for the information they collect, making financial regulatory data interoperable and fully searchable. (Source: [[Covington - FDTA Implementation]])

---

## What it requires

- **Joint data standards** across nine agencies: CFTC, OCC, CFPB, FDIC, FHFA, Federal Reserve, NCUA, **SEC**, and Treasury. (Source: [[Covington - FDTA Implementation]])
- Data must be **fully searchable, machine-readable, and non-proprietary / open-licensed**.
- **Common identifiers**, including a nonproprietary **Legal Entity Identifier (LEI)** - the agencies adopted ISO 17442 for the LEI and ISO 4914 for swap identification. (Source: [[SEC.gov - FDTA Joint Data Standards]])
- The SEC must expand machine-readable requirements, including data submitted to FINRA and the MSRB.
- Importantly, the FDTA **does not require collecting more information** than agencies already collect - it standardizes the format. (Source: [[Covington - FDTA Implementation]])

## Why it matters

FDTA is the statutory push that extends the [[XBRL]] structured-data philosophy beyond corporate financial statements to the whole federal financial-regulatory stack. For issuers and financial entities, the burden depends on the final standards adopted. (Source: [[Covington - FDTA Implementation]])
