# Regulation Crowdfunding

**Regulation Crowdfunding** (Reg CF) is the SEC exemption that lets private companies raise capital from the general public (including non-accredited investors) through online platforms. It implements **Title III of the JOBS Act of 2012**, with rules effective May 16, 2016. (Source: [[crowdfundingattorney.com - 2026 Reg CF Guidance]])

---

## Core rules

- **Issuer cap: $5 million** in any rolling 12-month period (raised from $1.07M effective March 15, 2021). (Source: [[SEC.gov - Regulation Crowdfunding]])
- **Must use a registered intermediary.** Every Reg CF transaction must run through an SEC-registered **[[Funding Portal]]** or a broker-dealer; an issuer cannot sell directly. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Regulation Crowdfunding]])
- **Investor limits.** Non-accredited investors are capped annually based on income and net worth; the overall ceiling is **$124,000 per year**. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Regulation Crowdfunding]])
- **Resale restriction.** Securities generally cannot be resold for **one year** after purchase.

## Filings on EDGAR

- **[[Form C]]** — filed before the offering goes live; discloses business, financials, use of proceeds, and risks.
- **Form C-AR** — annual report, due within **120 days** of fiscal year-end, filed on EDGAR and posted to the issuer's website.
- Annual reporting continues until a defined off-ramp (e.g., issuer becomes an Exchange Act reporter, falls below 300 holders after one report, or below $10M assets after three reports). (Source: [[SEC.gov - Regulation Crowdfunding]])

## Market scale

As of end-2024, there were **83 funding portals** registered with the SEC and FINRA. The market is concentrated: in 2025, Wefunder (~$109M, ~33% share), StartEngine (~$89M, ~24%), and DealMaker (~$66M) led Reg CF dollars raised. (Source: [[Angel Investors Network - Reg CF Platform Comparison]])

## Confidence

**high** on the dollar caps, intermediary requirement, and Form C/C-AR mechanics (corroborated by SEC.gov and multiple law sources); **medium** on 2025 platform-share figures (single market-analysis source).
