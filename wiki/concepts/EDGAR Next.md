# EDGAR Next

**EDGAR Next** is the SEC's 2024–2025 modernization of [[EDGAR]] filer access and account management - the most significant upgrade to the filing system in over 20 years. It replaces shared logins with individual, identity-verified, role-based access. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Transition to EDGAR Next]])

---

## What changes

- **Login.gov required.** Access to the EDGAR Filer Management dashboard requires individual Login.gov credentials with **multi-factor authentication**. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Transition to EDGAR Next]])
- **No more shared credentials.** Legacy passwords, passphrases, and PMACs are deactivated - each user authenticates individually. (Source: [[Nuvo Group - EDGAR Next Transition]])
- **Account administrators.** Each filer must designate **at least two account administrators** (except certain single-member entities) responsible for account management and annual confirmations. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Transition to EDGAR Next]])
- **Dashboard.** A new EDGAR Filer Management dashboard becomes the central hub for managing access and filing.
- **Machine-to-machine APIs.** API access for automated filing becomes available to filers who have enrolled or whose amended Form ID applications are approved. (Source: [[Nuvo Group - EDGAR Next Transition]])

## Why it matters

The change closes the credential-sharing security hole exposed in part by the 2017 EDGAR breach, and brings the filing system in line with modern federal identity standards (Login.gov + MFA). Filers who did not enroll by the deadline lost the ability to file until they completed enrollment.

## Confidence

**high** for the core mechanics and headline dates (multiple law-firm + SEC sources agree); **medium** for the precise December 2025 sub-deadlines.
