# Rule 144

**Rule 144** is the safe harbor that lets holders publicly **resell** restricted or control securities without registration, if conditions are met. It is how privately-acquired shares eventually become freely tradable. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Rule 144]])

---

## Two security types

- **Restricted securities:** acquired in unregistered private sales (e.g., a [[Regulation D]] placement).
- **Control securities:** held by **affiliates** (officers, directors, large shareholders) who influence the issuer.

## Conditions

- **Holding period:** at least **6 months** if the issuer is an SEC-reporting company; **1 year** if not. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Rule 144]])
- **Current public information** about the issuer must be available.
- **Affiliates face extra limits even after the holding period:**
  - **Volume limit:** in any 3 months, no more than the greater of **1% of the outstanding class** or the **average weekly trading volume** over the preceding 4 weeks.
  - **Manner-of-sale** requirements (brokers' transactions).
  - **Form 144** notice filing with the SEC.
- **Non-affiliates:** after the holding period (and current info), can resell **without** volume/manner-of-sale limits.

## Confidence

**high** - holding periods, the 1%/weekly-volume affiliate cap, and Form 144 are codified in the rule and corroborated across sources.
