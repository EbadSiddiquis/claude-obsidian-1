# U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission

The **SEC** is the U.S. federal agency that regulates securities markets and protects investors. It was established by the [[Federal Securities Laws|Securities Exchange Act of 1934]] and administers the body of federal securities law. (Source: [[investor.gov - Laws That Govern the Securities Industry]])

---

## Mandate

The SEC's stated mission is to protect investors, maintain fair and orderly markets, and facilitate capital formation. Its core regulatory tool is **mandatory disclosure**: public companies must regularly file standardized information so investors can make informed decisions. (Source: [[investor.gov - Laws That Govern the Securities Industry]])

## What it regulates

- Public company disclosure (annual, quarterly, and event-driven reporting)
- Securities offerings and registration
- Securities exchanges, brokers, and dealers
- Investment companies (mutual funds) and investment advisers
- Insider trading and securities fraud enforcement

## Key infrastructure

The SEC operates [[EDGAR]] (Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval), the electronic system through which all mandatory filings are submitted and made public. As of 2025, EDGAR is undergoing its largest modernization in over 20 years - [[EDGAR Next]]. (Source: [[Wikipedia - EDGAR]])
