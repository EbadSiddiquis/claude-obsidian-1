# Financial Accounting Standards Board

**FASB** is the private-sector body that sets U.S. Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (US GAAP) and maintains the **US GAAP Financial Reporting Taxonomy** used for [[XBRL]] tagging in SEC filings. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Inline XBRL]])

---

- Maintains the ~17,000-tag **US GAAP Taxonomy** that defines the meaning of each financial-statement concept filers tag against. (Source: [[SEC.gov - Inline XBRL]])
- The SEC accepts and references FASB's taxonomy in EDGAR; FASB updates it roughly annually.
- FASB sets the accounting standards (US GAAP); the [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|SEC]] has statutory authority over public-company accounting and recognizes FASB standards.
